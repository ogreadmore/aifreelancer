Part 1: What & Why (Client & Manager View)

Quick Story — What will happen

Overview: We’ll define content pillars and your voice, generate a calendar of posts with caption variants and assets, run them through approvals, and schedule consistently. Each week we review performance and iterate on hooks, pillars, and CTAs.

What each step means (plain):

  • Pillars & voice: choose 3–6 themes and a short voice guide (dos/don’ts).
  • Calendar: decide cadence per channel (e.g., 4 posts/week) and approvers.
  • Production: draft 30 ideas with 2–3 caption variants each; create assets; add alt text.
  • Scheduling: set optimal times, add UTM links, and queue posts.
  • Optimization: test hooks and pillar mix; keep what works.
  • Governance: enforce deny‑lists and approval SLAs; archive rights/attribution.

For you — the person doing the work:

  • Draft a one‑page voice & pillars guide; confirm cadence and approvers.
  • Produce ideas, captions, and assets with accessibility checks.
  • Schedule with UTMs; set alerts for brand‑risk terms before posting.
  • Report weekly winners/losers; propose 2 experiments for next week.

For the client — what it should feel like:

  • Easy: approve a clear calendar; no last‑minute rushes.
  • Helpful: content stays on‑brand and relevant to audiences.
  • Safe: deny‑list, approvals, and rights checks reduce risk.
  • Clear: weekly notes show results and next tests.

Communication & Reporting

  • Weekly content review (30–45 min): approve upcoming calendar, confirm experiments, adjust tone/CTAs, resolve blockers.
  • Weekly status note (Fri): shipped posts, best/worst performers, experiments run, next week plan, approvals needed.
  • Monthly insights deck (5–8 slides): pillar mix, hook performance, growth, leads by UTM, next bets.
  • Alerting: Slack/Email for brand‑risk keywords, broken links, missed posts, auth issues.
  • Dashboard: cadence, engagement rate, CTR, follower growth, and leads; filter by pillar and platform.
Newbie guide: What to look for in the weekly review

Focus on hooks (first line), visuals (thumbnail/first frame), and CTA. If a post underperformed, try a new hook or a different asset before scrapping the idea.

Keep a simple scorecard: Hook strength (1–5), Asset fit (1–5), CTA clarity (1–5). Improve the lowest score first.

Step 1 — Plan

Newbie guide: Pillars & cadence

Pillars: Choose 3–6 themes you can post about every week (e.g., how‑to tips, customer stories, behind‑the‑scenes, offers). Rotate them to keep variety.

Cadence: Pick a schedule you can keep. It’s better to post 3×/week consistently than 10× once and then stop.

Approvals: Decide who approves what and how fast. Set a 24–48h SLA and a backup approver so the queue keeps moving.

Step 2 — Produce

Newbie guide: Writing great captions

Use this structure: Hook (1 line) → Value (1–3 lines) → CTA (what to do next).

Examples of hooks: “Most people get X wrong…”, “3 ways to Y this week”, “We almost quit because…”.

Always add alt text for images and subtitles for videos. Add UTM tags to links so you can see which post drove traffic.

Step 3 — Optimize

Newbie guide: Simple A/B tests

Only change one thing at a time (hook, image, or CTA). Post at similar times. Keep a small log: date, change, result. Keep winners; archive losers.

Don’t trust tiny samples; wait for at least a few hundred impressions per variant.

Step 4 — Govern

Newbie guide: Safety & rights

Never make claims that require proof unless you have a source. If a post is sponsored or uses affiliate links, disclose it (e.g., “#ad”).

Track asset rights (stock licenses, creator permissions) and keep them in a shared folder. When in doubt, do not post.

Delivery Phases

  1. Discovery: Audit channels, competitors, audience insights, past analytics.
  2. Strategy: Pillars, hooks, voice, cadence, CTA framework.
  3. Production: Caption variants, assets, alt text, approvals.
  4. Scheduling: Optimal times, UTM + tracking, cross‑posting.
  5. Optimize: Weekly insights, test hooks, iterate pillars.

Stage Gates

  • Voice guide signed off (tone traits, do/don’t examples)
  • Calendar v1 approved (≥ 2 weeks scheduled)
  • Accessibility checklist pass (alt text, captions)
  • UTM scheme agreed (source/medium/campaign)
  • Moderation plan live (reply policy + escalation)

Risks & Mitigations

  • Brand risk: deny‑list; 2‑person review for sensitive topics
  • Inconsistent cadence: 2–3 week backlog; approval SLAs + backups
  • Low engagement: test 3 hook patterns/week; rotate pillars; audit visuals
  • Rights issues: track licenses; whitelist UGC sources; approval needed for reposts

Voice & Branding

  • Define 3–5 tone attributes; include prohibited phrases.
  • Create post patterns: educational, proof, behind‑the‑scenes, CTA.
  • Platform guidelines: character counts, hashtags, link placement.
  • Asset spec sheet; ensure alt text and captions for accessibility.

KPIs & Reporting

  • Consistency: posts/week vs. target (goal ≥ 4/wk).
  • Engagement rate: (likes + comments + saves + shares) ÷ impressions (goal +25% vs. baseline).
  • Click‑through rate (CTR): link clicks ÷ impressions (track by platform and pillar).
  • Traffic & leads: UTM sessions, session quality (time, pages), conversions.
  • Content winners: top pillars/hooks; replicate formats and CTAs.

Glossary (Newbie‑friendly)

Pillars: 3–6 themes you post about consistently (e.g., tips, stories, product).

Hook: The first line that grabs attention; short and punchy.

Alt text: A short description for images so screen readers can describe them.

UTM: Tags added to links so analytics can attribute traffic correctly.

Part 2: Implementer's Guide

This section contains the detailed, technical step-by-step procedures for execution.

Initial Setup (SOP)

  1. Draft one‑page voice & pillars; define approvers and cadence per channel.
  2. Set up a content calendar (Notion/Sheets) with status, owner, and due dates.
  3. Create asset library structure; define naming and rights/attribution policy.
  4. Define UTM conventions (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign) and add to scheduler templates.
  5. Prepare 10 evergreen posts to seed the backlog and prevent cadence gaps.
  6. Set up alerting for brand‑risk terms and link checks before publishing.

Access & Prerequisites

  • Admin access to scheduling tool (Buffer/Later/Meta Business Suite).
  • Brand kit (logo, colors, fonts), product shots, and rights status.
  • Link shortener / UTM policy and analytics access.
  • Platform access for all channels; 2FA enabled.
  • Compliance guidelines: disclaimers, claims policy, sponsorship disclosure.

Design Notes

  • Accessibility: alt text, readable contrast, captions for video; avoid flashing.
  • Approvals: deny‑list of risky terms; two‑person review for high‑risk posts.
  • Variations: generate 2–3 caption variants per idea; test hooks and CTAs.
  • Visual fit: platform‑specific crops; safe areas for text; consistent thumbnail style.
  • Voice consistency: examples of “say this, not that” for clarity.

Platform nuances (cheat sheet)

Instagram: Visual first; use alt text; hashtags (3–10) relevant; links in bio.

LinkedIn: Lead with a strong first line; avoid walls of hashtags; tag people/companies thoughtfully.

TikTok/Reels: Hook in 1–2 seconds; big captions; on‑screen text + subtitles; trend sounds with care.

X (Twitter): Short hooks; 1–2 hashtags max; threads for depth.

Security & Compliance

  • Rights: use licensed/original assets; document creator permissions and expiry.
  • Claims: avoid unverifiable promises; link to proof; add disclaimers where needed.
  • Disclosures: mark sponsored or affiliate posts (#ad, #sponsored) per platform policy.
  • Accessibility: alt text for images, captions for videos, readable contrast; avoid flashing content.
  • Account security: SSO, role‑based access, 2FA, audit log reviews quarterly.
