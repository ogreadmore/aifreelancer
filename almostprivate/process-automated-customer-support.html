⚡ Core Tier — Automated Customer Support

Launch quickly with reliable answers from your help center, polite handoffs, and basic alerting. Minimal integrations; strong safety defaults.

✨ Quick Story — What will happen

We connect your assistant to your help articles, add guardrails, test with real questions, and soft‑launch. Unanswered or low‑confidence questions are routed to a human with context. Weekly updates and a monthly KPI snapshot keep everyone aligned.

Newbie guide: How this works end‑to‑end

Story mode: You will pick the most‑used help pages, tell the assistant to only use those, and try real questions. If it’s not sure, it passes the conversation to a human with a short summary. When it looks good for a day or two, you turn it on for everyone.

  1. List top help pages (password reset, billing, shipping, plan changes).
  2. Point the assistant to those pages and require it to show the source link.
  3. Try 20–30 real questions; if answers are weak, improve the page or rules.
  4. Soft‑launch to a small audience; if stable for 48h, roll out to all users.

💬 Communication & Reporting

  • Blueprint: 1‑page plan after discovery. Approval gates build.
  • Cadence: Weekly updates (Done, Next, Risks/Needs) in Slack/Email.
  • Visibility: Tiny Kanban (To‑Do/Doing/Done). Client access.
  • Reporting: Monthly snapshot: deflection, SLA, CSAT, top intents.
Newbie guide: What do we send each week?

Story mode: Send one short note with three parts: what we finished, what’s next, and what we need (e.g., a missing article). Attach one small chart of deflection or CSAT.

Step 1 — Baseline & Goals

Newbie guide: Taking a baseline

Story mode: Download last 30 days of support data and write the numbers on a one‑pager. This proves improvement later.

  1. Export CSAT, FRT/ASA, AHT, FCR, and top intents/volumes.
  2. Pick targets (e.g., deflection +35%, CSAT 4.6+, SLA 95%+).
  3. Note quiet hours and languages; confirm owner for escalations.
  4. Decide if you want to tag UTM on web chats for later analysis.

Step 2 — Knowledge Grounding

Newbie guide: Make answers reliable

Story mode: Copy final, public help pages into a search index. Keep a link to the exact page so answers can show the source.

  • Chunk by heading (~512 tokens) with small overlaps.
  • Remove boilerplate (nav/footers). Store title + URL per chunk.
  • Set a refresh schedule (e.g., daily) to catch new/edited pages.
  • Keep a gap list for unknowns; assign an owner and due date.

Step 3 — Guardrails & Handoff

Newbie guide: Play it safe

Story mode: Write simple house rules, then describe what to include when asking a human for help.

  • Do: Answer only from help pages; show the source link; ask clarifying questions.
  • Don’t: Never process refunds, reset credentials, or promise outcomes.
  • Handoff packet: 1‑line summary, intent, sentiment, confidence, links to cited pages, user email/phone normalized to E.164.
  • Data hygiene: De‑dupe by email/domain; log activity against the user/ticket.
  • Quiet hours: Don’t send messages overnight; queue them for morning.

Step 4 — Build & Embed

Newbie guide: First install

Story mode: Paste one small code snippet into a hidden page, ask common questions, and check that links open the right help articles. Always include a “Contact support” escape.

If sending emails, configure SPF/DKIM/DMARC on your domain to avoid spam folders.

Step 5 — Testing

Newbie guide: Check before launch

Story mode: Make a simple sheet of expected answers (with links). Mark responses as correct, needs work, or unsafe. Fix and re‑test.

  • Acceptance: ≥70% deflection on the test set; hallucination <3%.
  • Safety: no promises; always cite sources; offer human help if unsure.

Step 6 — Launch & Hypercare

Newbie guide: Watch closely

Story mode: Turn it on for one page or channel first. Watch logs for broken links, low confidence, or high handoffs. Fix and then roll out wider.

Step 7 — Ongoing Optimization

Newbie guide: Keep improving

Story mode: Read a few conversations each week. If something’s missing, add or update the help article and re‑index. Share a tiny monthly deck of results and next steps.

🚀 Enhanced Tier — Automated Customer Support

Adds guided workflows, routing, SLAs, enrichment, bi‑directional sync, and dashboards.

✨ Quick Story — What will happen

We design intent‑based routes, validate inputs, enrich contact data (time‑boxed), and sync with your ticketing/CRM. We add idempotency and retries, then run UAT including error paths before go‑live.

Newbie guide: How Enhanced differs

Story mode: You add small menus and buttons (“billing, technical, account”), collect details safely, send data to your system without creating duplicates, and check a dashboard that shows what’s working.

  1. Make a tiny menu of intents; confirm the data needed per path.
  2. Enrich contacts (company, plan) but stop after a few seconds.
  3. Send data with an idempotency key so repeats don’t duplicate tickets.
  4. Test success and failure cases (timeouts, 429/5xx) before launch.

💬 Communication & Reporting

Weekly updates; integration change log; monthly deck with deflection by intent and SLA trends.

Newbie guide: Monthly deck

Story mode: Export three charts (deflection by intent, SLA compliance, top questions). Add two wins, two fixes, and three next actions with owners.

Step 1 — Discovery & Routing

Newbie guide: Routes and fields

Story mode: Create a small menu and list what you must collect for each path. Validate inputs before sending.

  • Pricing/billing: email (regex), plan, last 4 digits if policy allows.
  • Technical: product area, screenshot link; require consent before logs.
  • Normalize phone numbers to E.164 if collected; tag intent.

Step 2 — Knowledge + Enrichment

Newbie guide: Enrichment

Story mode: Add missing context (company size, plan, region) from a safe source. Stop after a few seconds so users aren’t waiting.

Score contacts (e.g., VIP plan → fast‑lane route) with simple rules.

Step 3 — Flows & Sync

Newbie guide: Safe syncing

Story mode: Include a unique request ID so repeated calls don’t create duplicate tickets. If the server is busy, try again after 1s, then 2s, then 4s (stop after a limit).

Step 4 — UAT & Error Paths

Newbie guide: Test failures too

Story mode: Pretend the server is slow or down and make sure users get a friendly message and the system retries later. Confirm dashboards show deflection and SLA by intent.

Step 5 — Launch & Review

Newbie guide: First month check‑in

Story mode: After launch, watch the dashboard daily for a week. Fix obvious misses (bad routes, invalid inputs). At 30 days, compare results to targets and adjust rules.

🏢 Enterprise Tier — Automated Customer Support

Add identity, change management, observability, data governance, and reliability engineering.

✨ Quick Story — What will happen

We enable SSO/RBAC, approvals, and feature flags with canaries. We sync to the warehouse with lineage and DLP, add observability and SLOs, and run incident drills. Monthly eval packs drive improvements.

Newbie guide: Enterprise controls

Story mode: People log in with company accounts, important changes require a second person, new features roll out to a few users first, and we watch live health numbers to catch issues early.

💬 Communication & Reporting

Quarterly roadmap; monthly eval pack; incident reviews; change advisory notes.

Step 1 — Identity & Access

Newbie guide: Who can do what

Story mode: Connect your identity provider so people use work accounts. Give each role only what it needs. Require a second person to approve sensitive changes.

  • Roles: Viewer, Agent, Builder, Admin (least privilege).
  • SCIM: auto‑create and deactivate users from HR.

Step 2 — Release Safety

Newbie guide: Safer launches

Story mode: Turn on changes for a few users first and watch dashboards. If healthy, expand to 25%, then 100%. Practice turning features off quickly.

Step 3 — Data & Observability

Newbie guide: See everything

Story mode: Send key events (questions, answers, handoffs) to the warehouse, tag them with where they came from, and set rules for masking and retention. Build dashboards to watch errors, speed, and costs.

Step 4 — Reliability & Incidents

Newbie guide: When things break

Story mode: Pick target reliability numbers (e.g., 99% answers under 3s). If you go over your error budget, slow down changes and fix the cause. Keep a simple checklist for outages.
