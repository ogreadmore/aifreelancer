Part 1: What & Why (Client & Manager View)

Quick Story — What will happen

We identify your highest‑leverage opportunities, prioritize them into a 90‑day roadmap, and deliver in weekly sprints. We set up guardrails (access, data policies), publish dashboards, and meet with leadership regularly to make decisions quickly.

What each step means (plain):

  • Charter & KPIs: write a one‑pager with vision, KPI tree (revenue, cost, risk), cadence, and roles.
  • Guardrails: agree on model use policy, retention, approvals, and incident runbook so work is safe.
  • Backlog: collect and size opportunities; pick the top 3 workstreams by value and effort.
  • Delivery: ship weekly, demo often, and measure impact; remove blockers fast.
  • Scale: standardize wins, document SOPs, and expand to more teams.
  • Review: publish ROI; refresh the roadmap; tune guardrails as the org matures.

For you — the person doing the work:

  • Draft the charter; align sponsors; set demo and steering cadences.
  • Stand up tracker/docs/dashboards; define Definition of Done and stage gates.
  • Run a backlog workshop; score by ROI; pick 3 workstreams; start delivery.
  • Report weekly: shipped items, adoption, incidents, decisions needed; publish monthly ROI.

For the client — what it should feel like:

  • Easy: clear ownership and simple checkpoints; no mystery work.
  • Helpful: demos show useful things working in the real environment.
  • Safe: access and data policies in place from day one; zero surprises.
  • Clear: ROI and risks are visible; decisions are fast; next steps obvious.

Step 1 — Charter & KPI Tree

Newbie guide: The charter

What it is: A one‑page map that explains the goal, how we’ll measure success, who decides, and how often we meet. Short on purpose so everyone reads it.

  • Vision: One sentence (example: “Reduce support response time by 50% with an AI copilot”).
  • KPIs: Pick 2–3 you can actually measure (e.g., avg handling time, tickets per agent, CSAT).
  • Roles: Sponsor (approves), Lead (runs day‑to‑day), Stakeholders (give feedback).
  • Cadence: Weekly demo (30 min), bi‑weekly steering (30 min), monthly ROI report.

Acceptance criteria: Sponsor signs the charter; the KPIs have owners; meeting invites are on the calendar.

Step 2 — Guardrails & Access

Newbie guide: Guardrails

Plain words: Guardrails are safety rules. They say who can use the system, what data is allowed, and what to do if there’s a problem.

  • Allowed use: Example: “No personal medical data. No uploading customer secrets.”
  • Approvals: Who approves a new data source or a new workflow.
  • Incident plan: If something goes wrong, who is called, what gets paused, and how to roll back.

Acceptance criteria: Policy doc posted; team can find it; everyone knows how to report an issue.

Step 3 — Backlog & Prioritization

Newbie guide: Backlog

Collect ideas from teams using a simple template: problem → idea → expected impact → effort → risks.

Score each: Impact (big or small), Confidence (how sure are we), Effort (how hard). High impact, high confidence, low effort = top picks.

Acceptance criteria: One list, top 3 chosen, and each has an owner and a Definition of Done.

Step 4 — Delivery Rhythm

Newbie guide: Rhythm

Weekly demo: 3 bullets — shipped, next, blockers. Share links, not slides. Keep it to 30 minutes.

Steering: Every 2 weeks, ask for decisions (approve, stop, change). Keep a decision log so you don’t re‑debate.

Acceptance criteria: Demos on calendar, decision log exists, blockers resolved within a week.

Step 5 — Ship Quick Wins

Newbie guide: MVP

Build the smallest version that solves one real task for a real user. Add simple logging so you can see if people use it.

  • Start with one team and one workflow.
  • Measure: how many people used it, how long it took, did it help?

Acceptance criteria: MVP usable by target users; usage and feedback captured; next iteration planned.

Step 6 — Prove ROI

Newbie guide: ROI

Compare the “before” numbers to the “after” numbers. Convert improvements into dollars using simple, explained math.

  • Time saved × hourly rate = cost saved.
  • Extra conversions × average deal size = revenue gained.
  • Fewer incidents × expected loss = risk avoided.

Acceptance criteria: 1‑page write‑up per workstream with baseline, after, method, and a conservative dollar value.

Step 7 — Scale & Institutionalize

Newbie guide: Scale

Turn the win into a recipe (SOP). Include screenshots, steps, who owns it, and how to get help.

Acceptance criteria: SOP published, owners assigned, and a plan to roll it out to the next team.

Communication & Reporting

  • Weekly: 30‑min demo; three bullets (shipped, next, needs) + unblockers.
  • Bi‑weekly: steering decisions; re‑prioritize backlog; risk review.
  • Monthly: ROI report (wins, adoption, metrics); update roadmap.

KPIs & ROI

  • ROI (90‑day): (Revenue gained + Cost saved + Risk avoided) / Program fees
  • Adoption: % of target users actively using shipped workflows
  • Velocity: Story points or outputs per sprint; trend over time
  • Stability: Incidents/severity; MTTR; change failure rate
  • Safety: Evals pass rate; policy exceptions; red‑team findings
How to measure
  • ROI: Keep a conservative impact sheet per workstream; update monthly.
  • Adoption: Track weekly active users and key task completions.
  • Velocity: Use tracker reports; watch for sustained, predictable throughput.
  • Stability: Incident log + MTTR dashboard; aim for downward trend.
  • Safety: Store eval runs and sign‑offs with each release.

Glossary (Newbie‑friendly)

CAIO: Fractional Chief AI Officer — strategic leader for AI.

Backlog: Prioritized list of potential work with value estimates.

MTTR: Mean time to resolve — how fast we fix issues.

Definition of Done: Clear checklist that must be true before work is “done”.

Guardrails: Simple safety rules for access, data, and approvals.

Part 2: Implementer's Guide

Detailed, technical procedures for execution.

Initial Setup (SOP)

  1. Draft and approve program charter (vision, objectives, KPI tree, cadence).
  2. Stand up working spaces: tracker (Linear/Jira), docs (Notion/Confluence), dashboards.
  3. Access: SSO groups, least‑privilege accounts, secrets management, audit logging.
  4. Backlog workshop: collect candidates, size, and prioritize by ROI and risk.
  5. Select 3 workstreams; define Definition of Done and stage gates.
  6. Schedule weekly demos and bi‑weekly steering syncs for decisions.

Access & Prerequisites

  • Stakeholder availability (weekly demos, steering)
  • Tooling licenses (tracker, docs, dashboards)
  • Data/source access for relevant workstreams (CRM, Ops tools, Warehouse)
  • Security approvals for model usage and integrations

Design Notes

  • Workstream balance: 1 revenue, 1 ops, 1 data/intelligence for risk‑balanced impact.
  • Adoption first: enablement, office hours, clear ownership drive real usage.
  • Decision velocity: maintain a visible decision log; escalate blockers within 48h.
  • Change management: announce, train, follow‑up; measure active usage.
  • Risk management: maintain risk log; treat policy as a living doc.

Security & Compliance

  • Policy: Program‑level rules for model use, retention, export controls, and human‑in‑the‑loop.
  • Vendors: Due diligence; data processor inventory; DPIAs where required.
  • Access: Monthly reviews; role‑based permissions; segregation of duties; audit logging.
  • Evals: Safety/quality evals (prompt injection, jailbreaks, bias) on each release.
  • Incidents: Severity, comms templates, rollback plan, and post‑mortems with actions.
