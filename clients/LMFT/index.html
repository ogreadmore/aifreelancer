Starting Your
Private Practice

A plain-language guide for licensed therapists in California

California has specific rules for therapists who want to incorporate. The good news: the process is straightforward and inexpensive. This guide walks you through the two core pieces — your legal entity and your public-facing brand name — so you can launch with the right foundation from day one.

1
Legal Structure

Form a Professional Corporation — not an LLC

California does not allow licensed therapists to use an LLC for their practice. Instead, you'll form a Professional Corporation (PC) — a structure designed specifically for licensed professionals like you. Think of it as the therapist's version of an LLC: it protects your personal assets, gives you a legal entity for billing and contracts, and is what insurance companies and payors expect to see.

The legal name must include words like family, marriage, or child paired with therapy, therapist, counseling, or counselor.

Lira Family Therapy, A Professional Corporation Recommended Legal Entity Name

This name is clean, professionally credible, legally compliant, and broad enough to grow with you into couples work, family therapy, or broader women's health — without needing to rename.

2
Public Brand

Choose a public-facing brand name

Your legal name is for paperwork. Your brand name is what clients actually see — on your website, Psychology Today profile, Google listing, and business cards. In California, this is called a Fictitious Business Name (FBN), filed with your county.

We recommend two names. The primary is your main brand. The secondary costs almost nothing to file alongside it, and covers the exact search terms women use when they need you.

★ Primary Brand
Bloom Perinatal & Fertility Therapy

Warm, memorable, and clearly signals your niche. Strong for your website, logo, and directory profiles.

Secondary (Optional)
Pregnancy, Postpartum & Fertility Therapy Center

Keyword-rich. Matches exactly how patients search on Google and therapy directories. Worth filing as a backup.

3
Filing Order

Do these steps in this order

  • Confirm you are fully licensed — not in your associate registration period. California prohibits associates from owning or operating a private practice.
  • Check name availability for both the corporation name and your chosen brand name at bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov. You can reserve the name for 60 days.
  • File your Articles of Incorporation with the California Secretary of State. Fee: $100.
  • File your Fictitious Business Name with Riverside County (Temecula is in Riverside County, not San Diego). Fee: ~$26. This should be done within 40 days of first doing business.
  • Publish your FBN notice in an approved local newspaper once a week for four consecutive weeks. Publication must begin after the county filing. Then file an affidavit of publication with the county to complete the process.
  • Do a basic trademark check to confirm no one else is using your chosen brand name nationally.
4
Important Note

One thing to be careful about

⚠️

Avoid using the word "Center" in your primary brand name unless you are operating a multi-clinician facility. For a solo practice, it can create the wrong impression — and California's BBS specifically states that a fictitious business name cannot be false, misleading, or deceptive. "Bloom Perinatal & Fertility Therapy" avoids this entirely. The secondary name uses "Center" but it should remain a secondary filing, not your main identity.
