Localized care

What is equine cryotherapy?

It is targeted cold therapy delivered to a specific area of the horse rather than a whole-body setup. Dan brings the mobile equipment to the barn and works around the horse, handler, and available space.

Use cases

When does it make sense?

Owners often ask about cryotherapy after hard rides, during show season, around hauling, for soft-tissue recovery routines, or for older horses that need additional comfort support.

Care team

Does it replace the vet?

No. Cryotherapy supports recovery and comfort routines. It is not a diagnosis, urgent care, or a replacement for your vet, farrier, body worker, chiropractor, or trainer.

Setup

What do you need at the barn?

A safe, quiet place to work, a handler who knows the horse, and enough room around the treatment area. Dan will confirm anything else before the visit.

Questions

Equine cryotherapy FAQs.

What areas can be treated?

Common focus areas include hocks, knees, fetlocks, pasterns, tendons, suspensories, shoulders, back, hindquarters, and neck.

Can older horses use it?

Yes. Many owners use localized cryotherapy as part of a comfort and mobility routine for older horses, with veterinary guidance when needed.

Can you come to a show barn?

Yes, by request. Barn days, trainer partnerships, and multiple-horse visits can be quoted before booking.

How long does a visit take?

Timing depends on the horse, the treatment area, and the barn setup. Dan will give you a clear expectation before the appointment.

Do you need my vet involved?

If the horse has a new injury, active medical issue, or unclear symptoms, involve your vet first. Mobile Cryo Pro is recovery support, not veterinary care.

How do I book?

Call, text, or send the contact form with the horse’s location, what area you want addressed, and whether other providers are already involved.
