Equine FAQs

Before booking a barn-side visit.

Is this the same as human cryotherapy?

The idea is similar, but the visit is built around the horse, the handler, and the barn environment. Treatment stays localized to the area that needs support.

Does it replace a veterinarian?

No. Mobile Cryo Pro supports comfort and recovery routines. It does not diagnose injuries or replace veterinary care, farrier work, chiropractic care, or body work.

What areas can be treated?

Common focus areas include hocks, knees, fetlocks, pasterns, tendons, suspensories, shoulders, back, hindquarters, and neck.

What do you need at the barn?

A safe, quiet place to work, a handler who knows the horse, and enough room around the treatment area. Dan will confirm setup details before the visit.

Can older horses benefit?

Many equine clients use localized cryotherapy for aging horses, working horses, and performance horses that need additional recovery support between harder efforts.

How do I book?

Call, text, or send the form with the horse’s location, the area of concern, and whether your vet or trainer is already involved.
