The Science

Why the right ingredients break the itch cycle

Sweet itch is an allergic response to midge bites. The body overreacts, triggering intense inflammation — and that inflammation is what makes horses rub compulsively, breaking the skin and inviting secondary infections that make everything worse.

Medica Healing Salve targets all three stages at once. Calendula oil and lavandin essential oil deliver clinically recognized anti-inflammatory and soothing action that calms the response on contact. Medical-grade sulfur eliminates the bacteria and fungus that colonize broken skin. And the salve's protective barrier locks out further irritants while the skin repairs itself beneath it.

That's how the cycle breaks — not by masking the itch from the outside, but by treating the inflammation and infection driving it.

Common Mistake

Why managing sweet itch isn't the same as treating it

Fly rugs and insect repellents are a sensible first line of defense — but they only reduce exposure. They don't treat the skin that's already inflamed, rubbed raw, and often secondarily infected by the time you're reaching for them.

Antihistamines and steroids can reduce the systemic reaction, but they don't heal the damaged skin barrier or address the bacteria and fungus that move in once it's broken. Horses keep rubbing because the inflammation and irritation at the skin level are still there — unaddressed.
