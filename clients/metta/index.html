01

Find it

Bring to mind the being who evokes the strongest feeling of love in you. No anchor is too humble.

02

Strengthen it

Give the feeling your full attention and turn it all the way up — a mantra you feel rather than say.

03

Expand it

Keep the same felt wish and let it include more: yourself, a neutral person, a difficult person, all beings.

04

Return to it

Standing, walking, sitting, lying down — when you notice it has slipped away, return. Each return makes it home.

Asked & answered.

Is this religious?

No religious commitment is required or requested. The book treats the Metta Sutta as what it is — a Buddhist poem gathering a practice older than Buddhism — and teaches the practice in plain language. Readers of any tradition, and of none, practice from the same pages.

I've never meditated. Can I start here?

Yes — the book assumes nothing. It was written to be the first book, readable in an evening, with a practice that begins at one sincere minute.

What exactly do I get, and when?

Buying the book delivers the complete ebook (EPUB and PDF), the three guided recordings, and the Practice Card to your inbox within minutes. The class adds seven modules, twenty recordings, and live group sits with the author. The hardcover arrives on Amazon soon.

How does the guarantee work?

Who wrote it?

Taylor Oliphant — a long-time metta practitioner trained primarily in the tradition of Ajahn Sona of Birken Forest Monastery. He wrote the book after living and studying in Nepal for three years, and the translation of the Metta Sutta at its center is his own. Every claim is sourced; all 39 endnotes were verified against their publishers.
