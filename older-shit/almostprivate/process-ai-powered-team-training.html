Step 1 ‚Äî Kickoff & Goals

Newbie guide: What happens in kickoff?

  2. KPIs: choose self‚Äëservice %, speed‚Äëto‚Äëanswer, and logging coverage.
  3. Guardrails: topics to avoid (legal advice, personal HR issues), brand voice, and escalation paths.
  4. Quiet hours: set the local time window when alerts must not fire.

Step 2 ‚Äî Content Intake (Capture ‚Üí Upsert)

Newbie guide: Importing and cleaning

Story mode: Work from a simple spreadsheet: columns for URL, owner, topic, and status. Paste in the latest SOP links, strike through drafts, and tag each one by topic so people can find things later.

  1. Open each SOP; confirm it‚Äôs the latest. If not, link to the final version and archive the old one.
  2. De‚Äëdupe: treat same URL with small edits as one record; keep only the newest.
  3. Tag: Onboarding, Access, Sales, Support, Finance, etc. Add team/intent if useful.
  4. Normalize contacts: if you collect phone numbers, convert to E.164; for emails, lowercase and trim.
  5. Upsert: load or update the SOP into the assistant so it‚Äôs searchable and cited.

Done when: your sheet has no drafts, every SOP has an owner, and search finds the right doc by topic keywords.

  3. Routing map: topic ‚Üí owner email or channel. Test one unknown and confirm alert delivery.
  4. Logging: confirm each answer and each route is written to the log with a timestamp.

  4. Sign‚Äëoff: when 90% of pilot tasks work first‚Äëtry, move to launch.

Step 1 ‚Äî Discovery & Routing

Newbie guide: Routing and SLAs

Story mode: Draw a simple map: intent ‚Üí destination ‚Üí timer. Then test it like a user.

Step 2 ‚Äî Enrichment & Scoring

Newbie guide: Enrichment basics

Story mode: Enrichment should be tiny and time‚Äëboxed. Pull just enough data (team, location, manager) to make routing smarter ‚Äî nothing more.

  2. Configure a quick lookup (e.g., HRIS) with a 1‚Äì2s timeout and a safe fallback.
  3. Apply fast‚Äëlane: urgent intents or VIP teams jump the queue within SLA rules.

  4. Sign‚Äëoff: all must be green; attach logs and screenshots to the UAT sheet.

Step 1 ‚Äî Identity & Access

Newbie guide: Single sign‚Äëon

Story mode: Start with SSO and SCIM. Your identity team gives you a client ID/secret or a SAML config. You map groups to roles, and SCIM keeps accounts in sync.

  3. Enable SCIM and create a test account via provisioning; then de‚Äëprovision it.
  4. Confirm audit logs record login, role changes, and de‚Äëprovision events.

Step 2 ‚Äî Approvals & Change

Newbie guide: Safe changes

Story mode: Treat changes like air traffic control. One person builds, another approves, a flag controls exposure, and you can roll back in one click.

  1. Create a change ticket with the why, the flag name, and rollback steps.
  2. Run a canary: 5% for 2h ‚Üí 25% for 6h ‚Üí 100% if error rate and latency are green.
  3. Document outcomes in a change log; link to dashboards and logs.

Step 3 ‚Äî Data & Warehouse

Newbie guide: Data and privacy

Story mode: Build a thin, reliable pipe of events to your warehouse, with clear names and privacy rules.

  1. Define an event schema (who, what, when, where); version it.
  2. Add lineage: store the source system and processing steps for each event.
