Step 1 ‚Äî Discover & Map

Newbie guide: What is a process map?

Story mode: It's a simple drawing that shows how work gets done today. We'll identify the starting point (like "a new form is submitted"), each step ("copy the email to the spreadsheet"), and the end result ("send a Slack notification"). This helps us see exactly what to automate.

Step 2 ‚Äî Design & Approve

Newbie guide: What is data mapping?

Story mode: It's just a list that connects the dots. For example: "The 'Email Address' from the form goes into the 'Contact Email' column in our spreadsheet." This ensures the automation puts the right information in the right place.

Step 3 ‚Äî Build & Test

Newbie guide: How do we test it?

Story mode: We'll use a fake form submission to make sure the automation runs correctly. We'll check that the data appears in the right place and that the notification looks correct. We'll also intentionally cause an error to make sure the alert email gets sent.

Step 4 ‚Äî Launch & Monitor

Newbie guide: What are run logs?

Story mode: It's a history list that shows every time the automation ran. We can see if it was successful or if there was an error. We watch this closely after launch to make sure everything is working as expected.

Step 1 ‚Äî Discover & Design

Newbie guide: What's an idempotency key?

Story mode: It's like a unique order number. If the automation tries to process the same request twice (e.g., due to a network hiccup), it sees the same "order number" and knows not to create a duplicate. This prevents mistakes like charging a customer twice.

Step 2 ‚Äî Build & Instrument

Newbie guide: What's a dead-letter queue?

Story mode: It's a safety net. If a workflow fails multiple times and the automation gives up, it sends the failed job to a special "fix-it" list instead of deleting it. This way, we can investigate what went wrong and manually fix it without losing any data.

Step 3 ‚Äî UAT & Testing

Newbie guide: What is UAT?

Story mode: User Acceptance Testing. This is where we ask the people who actually use the process to try out the automation in a test environment. They follow a script to confirm it works as expected and handles errors gracefully before we launch it for real.

Step 4 ‚Äî Launch & Optimize

Newbie guide: After launch

Story mode: We'll turn on the automation for a small set of transactions first to ensure everything is smooth. We'll watch the dashboards closely for any red flags. Each month, we'll share a short report on performance and suggest ways to make it even better.

üìö Glossary (Newbie‚Äëfriendly)

Step 1 ‚Äî Governance & Architecture

Newbie guide: What are SLOs?

Story mode: Service Level Objectives. They are specific, measurable reliability targets we set for ourselves. For example, "The automation should successfully complete 99.9% of runs." This is more precise than just saying "it should be reliable" and helps us know when we need to focus on stability versus new features.

Step 2 ‚Äî Build & Secure

Newbie guide: What is CI/CD?

Story mode: Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment. It's an automated way to test and release changes. When we update the workflow, an automated process runs tests, asks for approval, and then deploys it to the right environment. This makes releases faster and safer than doing it all by hand.

Step 3 ‚Äî Testing & Release

Newbie guide: What are feature flags?

Story mode: It's like a light switch for a new feature. We can deploy the code with the new feature "off," then turn it on for a small group of users (e.g., 5%) to see how it performs. If everything looks good, we can gradually increase it to 100%. If there's a problem, we can instantly switch it off without having to redeploy everything.

Step 4 ‚Äî Operate & Govern

Newbie guide: What's a runbook?

Story mode: It's a checklist for handling a specific alert or incident. If the on-call person gets an alert at 2 AM, they can open the runbook and follow a step-by-step guide to diagnose and fix the problem quickly, even if they're not an expert on that specific workflow.
